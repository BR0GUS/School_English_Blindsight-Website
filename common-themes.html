Common Themes

  • The nature of consciousness: This theme explores the question of what it means to be conscious and how consciousness arises in the brain. The book discusses the concept of the "zombie agent," which is a being that appears to be conscious but lacks true self-awareness.
  • The impact of technology: The book examines the ways in which technological advances can both improve and complicate our lives. It also considers the potential consequences of developing advanced artificial intelligence and the ethical implications of using such technology.
  • Extraterrestrial life: Blindsight explores the possibility of intelligent life existing beyond Earth and the potential consequences of making contact with such life. The book raises questions about how we would communicate with and understand beings that may have fundamentally different ways of thinking and perceiving the world.
  • Ethics and morality: The book touches on ethical and moral issues related to the use of technology and the potential consequences of contact with extraterrestrial life. It considers the idea that our moral values may not be universal and that other beings may have different moral systems.

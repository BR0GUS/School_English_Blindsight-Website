About Me

Blindsight is a science fiction novel by Peter Watts that follows the crew of the starship Theseus as they embark on a mission to investigate a mysterious signal coming from the edge of the solar system. The crew includes a linguist named Siri Keeton, who has undergone a radical brain surgery that allows him to process information from multiple sources simultaneously, as well as a group of genetically engineered "teleopaths" who are able to communicate with extraterrestrial life.

As the crew approaches the source of the signal, they encounter a massive, apparently derelict spaceship that is orbiting a rogue planet. The ship is inhabited by a group of intelligent vampires who are able to manipulate the crew's perceptions and memories. The vampires reveal that they are the remnants of a highly advanced civilization that was destroyed by a weaponized form of the "Blindsight" effect, which is the phenomenon of being able to perceive and react to stimuli without being consciously aware of it.

The vampires offer to share their technology with the humans, but the crew is divided on whether or not to accept their offer. Some members argue that the technology could be used to protect humanity from similar threats in the future, while others believe that it is too dangerous and that it would be better to destroy the ship and its occupants. As the crew debates what to do, they are forced to confront their own biases and preconceptions about the nature of consciousness and the role of technology in society.

Ultimately, the crew is faced with a difficult decision that will have far-reaching consequences for both humanity and the vampires. Blindsight is a thought-provoking and thought-provoking novel that explores a wide range of complex themes, including the nature of consciousness, the impact of technological advances on society, and the potential consequences of contact with extraterrestrial life.

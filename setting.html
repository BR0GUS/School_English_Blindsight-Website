Setting

Blindsight is a science fiction novel by Peter Watts, first published in 2006. It is set in the near future, where humanity has made contact with several extraterrestrial species and has developed technology to allow humans to explore the far reaches of space.

The story follows a group of scientists and military personnel who are sent on a mission to investigate a mysterious object that has appeared on the edge of the solar system. The object, dubbed the "Fireflies," is believed to be a remnant of a long-dead alien civilization. The team, led by the protagonist, Siri Keeton, consists of a diverse group of individuals, each with their own unique abilities and flaws.

As they journey to the Fireflies, the team faces numerous challenges and dangers, including hostile alien life forms, the dangers of space travel, and their own personal demons. Along the way, they also discover disturbing secrets about the true nature of the Fireflies and the role they may have played in the history of the universe.

The novel explores themes of identity, consciousness, and the nature of intelligence, as well as the consequences of technological advancement and the ethical dilemmas it can create. It is a complex and thought-provoking work that has received critical acclaim for its innovative and challenging ideas.
